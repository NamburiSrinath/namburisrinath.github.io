Research Journey (Unfiltered)

If you landed to this page, it means you are interested to know more about “How to build a research profile?” or to know about my journey.

Truth be told, I am not the right guy to get inspiration from. I have this inferior complex and while I don't compare with others, I always look high of my peer accomplishments and try to get inspiration and push myself harder to improve myself and my skills.

That being said, there's a lot of serendipity and perseverance that goes on in building a research profile. The ability to handle rejections is a true skill that a researcher will develop over the course of hisher journey. Occasionally, I take break from all kinds of work. And my friends (who are fellow researchers) always suggest to focus on building skills and doing good work rather than targetting for a publication as a good work always results in a good paper (or a good patentgood research blog post). While I agree to his advice, given this fast-paced field, the sheer amount of pressure/expectations being built around the community makes it even more difficult to stay at top of the field.

In short, I don't have any particular advice. Neither am I qualified enough to provide any such advice. At a bare minimum, this page should serve as a gentle reminder of the journey that I went through to become who I am now, to show how perseverance and serendipity is very important in building an overall profile and to be grateful for all the things that happened along my journey.

Note: As such, I wanted to keep the unfiltered truth. Obviously, this will be filtered quite enough in my professional resume (which I believe will be done by almost every person). I wanted to bring out this raw version to reflect on my journey and stay strong when needed.
